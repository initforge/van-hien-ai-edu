# Văn Học AI — User Guide

> How to use Văn Học AI — Vietnamese Literature Learning Platform

**Version:** 1.0.0 | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-08

---

## 1. What Is This?

Văn Học AI is an AI-powered platform for learning Vietnamese literature. Instead of just reading texts and answering comprehension questions, students **talk directly to literary characters**, explore branching storylines, and receive AI-powered feedback on their work.

> **For non-technical readers:** Imagine being able to ask Chí Phèo (a character from Nam Cao's famous story) why he became the way he is — and getting a real, in-character response. Then imagine the same story, but with a different choice at the end. What if Chí Phèo never met Thị Nở? That's what the Multiverse feature does.

## 2. Features Overview

### 2.1 Character Chat
**What it does:** Students chat in real-time with AI-powered literary characters.

Characters are grounded in real works — Chí Phèo knows his biography, motivation, and era. Responses stream word-by-word, making the conversation feel alive.

**How to use:**
1. Go to a work in the Library
2. Click on a character
3. Type a message in the chat box
4. Read the streaming response in-character

**Result:** A full conversation history is saved. Students can review their chat later.

---

### 2.2 Multiverse Storylines
**What it does:** Branching narrative paths let students explore "what if" scenarios.

A teacher generates multiple alternative endings for a literary work. Students can travel to any branch and read a new continuation of the story.

**How to use:**
1. Go to **Multiverse** from the student dashboard
2. Select a literary work (e.g., "Chí Phèo")
3. Browse the available story branches
4. Click into any branch to read the alternate storyline

**Result:** A branching tree of alternative story paths, grouped by student and class.

---

### 2.3 Exam Room
**What it does:** Students take exams with auto-save.

Every answer is saved automatically as the student types. There's no "submit" panic — work is preserved even if the browser closes.

**How to use:**
1. Go to **Exam Room** from the student dashboard
2. Select the exam assigned by the teacher
3. Read the text/prompt, then answer the questions
4. Answers auto-save as you type
5. Submit when done

**Result:** A completed exam submitted for grading. AI provides per-criterion feedback.

---

### 2.4 Results & AI Feedback
**What it does:** Students view their exam results with AI-generated per-criterion scores and comments.

**How to use:**
1. Go to **Results** from the student dashboard
2. Select a submission
3. View the rubric scores and AI comments per criterion
4. Click on any criterion to see the full AI feedback

**Result:** Clear, actionable feedback on what was done well and what needs improvement.

## 3. Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### 3.1 First-Time Setup

1. Go to the platform URL
2. Click **Đăng nhập** (Login)
3. Enter your class invite code (provided by your teacher)
4. You'll be enrolled automatically — no password needed for MVP

### 3.2 Chatting with a Character

1. Log in as a student
2. Go to **Thư viện** (Library)
3. Click on any literary work
4. Click **Chat nhân vật** (Chat with character)
5. Select a character (e.g., Thị Nở)
6. Type your question and press Enter
7. Read the response
8. Continue the conversation — history is saved

### 3.3 Taking an Exam

1. Log in as a student
2. Go to **Phòng thi** (Exam Room)
3. Find your assigned exam
4. Click **Bắt đầu** (Start)
5. Read the work excerpt or prompt
6. Answer each question (auto-saves every few seconds)
7. Click **Nộp bài** (Submit) when finished
8. Wait for AI grading (typically 5-30 seconds)

### 3.4 Exploring the Multiverse

1. Log in as a student
2. Go to **Đa Vũ trụ** (Multiverse)
3. Browse available storylines by class and work
4. Click on any branch to read the alternate ending
5. See the comparison view to understand how the branch diverged

## 5. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Chat response is slow | Check your internet connection. First message after idle may take longer (AI cold start). |
| Exam doesn't appear | Check with your teacher — the exam may not be assigned to your class yet. |
| Auto-save isn't working | Make sure you're connected to the internet. Refresh the page and check if your last typed text is still there. |
| Login fails | Verify you're using the correct invite code. Ask your teacher for a new code if yours has expired. |
| AI grades seem off | Ask your teacher to review the rubric — the AI grades against the rubric your teacher configured. |

## 6. FAQ

**Q: Is this a replacement for my literature teacher?**
**A:** No. This platform supports learning — the teacher remains the primary guide. AI provides feedback and character conversations; the teacher designs the rubric, creates exams, and makes final decisions on grades.

**Q: Who can see my chat conversations?**
**A:** Only you and the platform's AI models. Teachers see aggregate statistics, not individual chat logs (unless you submit them as part of an exam).

**Q: How does AI grading work?**
**A:** When you submit an exam, the AI reads your answer against a rubric (a structured grading guide your teacher configured). It gives a score and comment for each rubric criterion — not just one overall number.

**Q: Can I redo an exam?**
**A:** It depends on your teacher's settings. Some exams allow multiple attempts; others are one-time only. Ask your teacher.

**Q: What is the Multiverse?**
**A:** It's a collection of alternative storylines generated from the same literary work. Instead of one fixed ending, there are multiple "what if" branches. Think of it as a choose-your-own-adventure for literature.
